# Prepositions

## What is a Preposition?

A Preposition is a word that shows the relationship between a noun/pronoun and another word in a sentence.

It tells us:

- Place (Where?)
- Time (When?)
- Direction (Where to?)
- Movement
- Relationship

# Why are Prepositions Important?

Prepositions are used in almost every English sentence.

### Example

- I live in Nagpur.
- The meeting is at 10 AM.
- The book is on the table.

Without prepositions, sentences sound incorrect.

# 1. Prepositions of Time

The most important ones are:

```text
In
On
At
```

---

# IN (Time)

Use for:

- Months
- Years
- Seasons
- Long periods

### Examples

- In July
- In 2026
- In winter
- In the morning

---

## Sentences

- I was born in 1994.
- We will deploy in July.
- I study English in the morning.

---

# ON (Time)

Use for:

- Days
- Dates
- Special days

### Examples

- On Monday
- On Sunday
- On 15 August
- On my birthday

---

## Sentences

- The meeting is on Monday.
- We deployed on Sunday.
- I joined the company on 15 June.

---

# AT (Time)

Use for:

- Exact Time

### Examples

- At 7 PM
- At 10:30 AM
- At midnight
- At noon

---

## Sentences

- The meeting starts at 10 AM.
- I wake up at 6 AM.
- The deployment begins at midnight.

---

# Quick Time Rule

```text
IN  → Month / Year / Season

ON  → Day / Date

AT  → Exact Time
```

# 2. Prepositions of Place

Most important:

```text
In
On
At
Under
Over
Between
Among
Behind
Near
```

---

# IN (Place)

Used when something is inside an area or space.

### Examples

- In a room
- In India
- In Nagpur
- In a box

---

## Sentences

- I live in Nagpur.
- The file is in the folder.
- The application is running in Kubernetes.

---

# ON (Place)

Used when something is on a surface.

### Examples

- On the table
- On the wall
- On the screen

---

## Sentences

- The book is on the table.
- The report is on my desk.

---

# AT (Place)

Used for a specific location.

### Examples

- At home
- At the office
- At the airport

---

## Sentences

- I am at home.
- She is at the office.
- We are at the meeting room.

---

# IN vs ON vs AT

| Preposition | Usage          |
| ----------- | -------------- |
| In          | Inside an area |
| On          | On a surface   |
| At          | Specific point |

---

### Examples

- I live in India.
- The laptop is on the table.
- I am at the office.

---

# UNDER

Means below something.

### Examples

- The bag is under the table.
- The server is under maintenance.

---

# OVER

Means above something.

### Examples

- The fan is over the table.
- The bridge is over the river.

---

# BETWEEN

Used for two things.

### Examples

- The office is between the bank and the hospital.
- The issue is between the application and database.

---

# AMONG

Used for more than two things.

### Examples

- He was sitting among friends.
- The issue was found among multiple services.

---

# BEHIND

Means at the back of something.

### Examples

- The car is behind the building.
- The root cause was hidden behind the error.

---

# NEAR

Means close to.

### Examples

- My house is near the station.
- The office is near the airport.

---

# 3. Prepositions of Direction

Used to show movement.

---

# TO

Shows destination.

### Examples

- I am going to Pune.
- Send the report to me.
- Connect to the server.

---

# FROM

Shows starting point.

### Examples

- I came from Nagpur.
- The email came from the manager.
- Data flows from source to destination.

---

# INTO

Movement from outside to inside.

### Examples

- He walked into the room.
- Data was inserted into the database.

---

# ONTO

Movement onto a surface.

### Examples

- Put the laptop onto the table.

---

# Through

Movement from one side to another.

### Examples

- The request passed through the firewall.
- Data travels through the network.

---

# Across

Movement from one side to another.

### Examples

- We travelled across India.
- The issue affected servers across regions.

---

# 4. Other Important Prepositions

---

# FOR

Used for duration, purpose, benefit.

### Examples

- For two hours
- For learning English
- For the customer

---

## Sentences

- I worked for two hours.
- This document is for training.

---

# SINCE

Used for starting point in time.

### Examples

- Since Monday
- Since 2020
- Since morning

---

## Sentences

- I have been working since morning.
- We have been using AWS since 2021.

---

# WITH

Used to show togetherness or tools.

### Examples

- With my friend
- With Docker
- With Kubernetes

---

## Sentences

- I work with Linux.
- We deployed with Jenkins.

---

# BY

Used for deadline, method, or agent.

### Examples

- By tomorrow
- By email
- Created by Natraj

---

## Sentences

- Complete the task by Friday.
- Send it by email.

---

# During

Used while something is happening.

### Examples

- During the meeting
- During deployment

---

## Sentences

- The issue occurred during deployment.
- We discussed it during the meeting.

---

# Since vs For

## Since

Starting Point

- Since 2020
- Since Monday
- Since morning

---

## For

Duration

- For 2 hours
- For 3 days
- For 5 years

---

## Examples

- I have worked here since 2024.
- I have worked here for 2 years.
