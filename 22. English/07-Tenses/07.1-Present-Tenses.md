# Present Tenses

## What is a Tense?

A tense tells us the time of an action.

It shows whether an action happens:

* Now (Present)
* Before (Past)
* Later (Future)

---

## Hindi Meaning

Tense बताता है कि कोई कार्य कब हो रहा है।

---

## Marathi Meaning

Tense म्हणजे एखादी क्रिया कोणत्या काळात होत आहे हे दर्शवते.

---

# Types of Present Tenses

There are four Present Tenses:

1. Simple Present Tense
2. Present Continuous Tense
3. Present Perfect Tense
4. Present Perfect Continuous Tense

---

# Present Tense Overview

| Tense                      | Usage                           |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Simple Present             | Habit, Routine, Fact            |
| Present Continuous         | Action happening now            |
| Present Perfect            | Action completed recently       |
| Present Perfect Continuous | Action continuing for some time |

---

# 1. Simple Present Tense

## Usage

Used for:

* Daily routine
* Habits
* Facts
* General truths

---

## Formula

### Positive

```text
Subject + V1 + Object
```

### Examples

* I work in Infosys.
* You play cricket.
* We learn English.

---

## He / She / It Rule

Add s/es to the verb.

### Formula

```text
He/She/It + Verb(s/es)
```

### Examples

* He works in Infosys.
* She reads books.
* It runs properly.

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ He work in Infosys.

✅ He works in Infosys.

---

❌ She read books.

✅ She reads books.

---

# Negative Sentences

## Formula

```text
Subject + do/does + not + V1
```

### Examples

* I do not work on Sundays.
* We do not play cricket.
* He does not work remotely.

---

# Questions

## Formula

```text
Do/Does + Subject + V1 ?
```

### Examples

* Do you work in IT?
* Does he play cricket?
* Does she speak English?

---

# Office Examples

* I check emails every day.
* The manager reviews reports.
* We attend daily meetings.

---

# DevOps Examples

* Jenkins builds the project.
* Docker runs containers.
* Kubernetes manages pods.
* Terraform creates infrastructure.

---

# Time Words

Often used with:

* Always
* Usually
* Often
* Sometimes
* Never
* Every day

### Examples

* I always complete my work.
* We usually deploy on Friday.

---

# 2. Present Continuous Tense

## Usage

Used for actions happening right now.

---

## Formula

```text
Subject + is/am/are + Verb+ing
```

---

# Examples

* I am learning English.
* He is working.
* They are playing cricket.

---

# Negative

```text
Subject + is/am/are + not + Verb+ing
```

### Examples

* I am not sleeping.
* He is not working.
* They are not playing.

---

# Questions

```text
Is/Am/Are + Subject + Verb+ing ?
```

### Examples

* Are you learning English?
* Is he working?
* Are they studying?

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ I learning English.

✅ I am learning English.

---

❌ He working on the issue.

✅ He is working on the issue.

---

# Office Examples

* I am checking the logs.
* We are attending a meeting.
* The team is discussing the issue.

---

# DevOps Examples

* I am deploying the application.
* The server is restarting.
* We are monitoring the cluster.

---

# Time Words

Usually used with:

* Now
* Right now
* At the moment
* Currently

### Examples

* I am working now.
* We are deploying currently.

---

# 3. Present Perfect Tense

## Usage

Used for actions completed recently.

Result is important now.

---

## Formula

```text
Subject + has/have + V3
```

---

# Examples

* I have completed the task.
* We have deployed the application.
* He has fixed the issue.

---

# Has vs Have

### Have

Used with:

* I
* You
* We
* They

### Has

Used with:

* He
* She
* It

---

# Examples

* I have finished my work.
* They have completed testing.
* She has sent the email.

---

# Negative

```text
Subject + has/have + not + V3
```

### Examples

* I have not completed the task.
* He has not replied.

---

# Questions

```text
Has/Have + Subject + V3 ?
```

### Examples

* Have you completed the task?
* Has he fixed the issue?

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ I have completed yesterday.

✅ I completed it yesterday.

OR

✅ I have completed it.

---

# Office Examples

* I have sent the report.
* We have completed testing.
* The manager has approved the request.

---

# DevOps Examples

* We have deployed the release.
* Jenkins has completed the build.
* The team has fixed the incident.

---

# Time Words

Often used with:

* Already
* Just
* Yet
* Ever
* Never

### Examples

* I have already completed the task.
* We have just deployed the application.

---

# 4. Present Perfect Continuous Tense

## Usage

Used for actions that started in the past and are still continuing.

---

## Formula

```text
Subject + has/have been + Verb+ing
```

---

# Examples

* I have been learning English for six months.
* He has been working since morning.
* We have been waiting for an hour.

---

# Since vs For

## Since

Used for a starting point.

### Examples

* Since 2020
* Since Monday
* Since morning

---

## For

Used for a duration.

### Examples

* For 2 hours
* For 5 days
* For 6 months

---

# Examples

* I have been working here since 2024.
* We have been learning English for six months.

---

# Negative

```text
Subject + has/have not been + Verb+ing
```

### Examples

* I have not been studying lately.
* He has not been working today.

---

# Questions

```text
Has/Have + Subject + been + Verb+ing ?
```

### Examples

* Have you been learning English?
* Has he been working here?

---

# Office Examples

* I have been working on this task since morning.
* We have been waiting for approval.

---

# DevOps Examples

* We have been monitoring the servers for two hours.
* The team has been troubleshooting the issue since yesterday.

---

# Tense Comparison

## Simple Present

* I work in Infosys.

(Routine)

---

## Present Continuous

* I am working now.

(Action happening now)

---

## Present Perfect

* I have completed my work.

(Action completed)

---

## Present Perfect Continuous

* I have been working for two hours.

(Action continuing)

---

# Common English Mistakes

❌ He work in Infosys.

✅ He works in Infosys.

---

❌ I am work.

✅ I am working.

---

❌ He have completed.

✅ He has completed.

---

❌ I have been work for 2 hours.

✅ I have been working for 2 hours.

# Key Points

✅ Simple Present = Habit / Routine

✅ Present Continuous = Happening Now

✅ Present Perfect = Recently Completed

✅ Present Perfect Continuous = Started Earlier and Still Continuing

✅ Present Tenses are used daily in Speaking, Meetings, Emails, Interviews, and Office Communication.

✅ Master Present Tenses before learning Past and Future Tenses.
