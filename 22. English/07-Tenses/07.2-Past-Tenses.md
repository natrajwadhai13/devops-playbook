# Past Tenses

## What is a Past Tense?

Past Tense is used to talk about actions that happened in the past.

### Hindi Meaning

Past Tense का उपयोग उन कार्यों के लिए किया जाता है जो पहले हो चुके हैं।

### Marathi Meaning

भूतकाळात घडलेल्या क्रिया सांगण्यासाठी Past Tense वापरला जातो.

---

# Types of Past Tenses

There are four Past Tenses:

1. Simple Past Tense
2. Past Continuous Tense
3. Past Perfect Tense
4. Past Perfect Continuous Tense

---

# Past Tense Overview

| Tense                   | Usage                                             |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Simple Past             | Completed action in the past                      |
| Past Continuous         | Action in progress at a specific time in the past |
| Past Perfect            | Action completed before another past action       |
| Past Perfect Continuous | Action continuing before another past action      |

---

# 1. Simple Past Tense

## Usage

Used for actions that started and finished in the past.

---

## Formula

```text
Subject + V2 + Object
```

---

## Examples

* I worked yesterday.
* She visited Pune last week.
* We completed the deployment.

---

## Common Verb Forms

| V1       | V2        | V3        |
| -------- | --------- | --------- |
| Go       | Went      | Gone      |
| Eat      | Ate       | Eaten     |
| Write    | Wrote     | Written   |
| Work     | Worked    | Worked    |
| Complete | Completed | Completed |

---

## Negative Sentences

### Formula

```text
Subject + did not + V1
```

### Examples

* I did not work yesterday.
* He did not attend the meeting.
* We did not deploy the release.

---

## Questions

### Formula

```text
Did + Subject + V1 ?
```

### Examples

* Did you complete the task?
* Did he call you?
* Did they deploy the application?

---

## Common Mistakes

❌ I did not worked.

✅ I did not work.

---

❌ Did you completed the task?

✅ Did you complete the task?

---

# Office Examples

* I attended the meeting.
* The manager approved the request.
* We submitted the report.

---

# DevOps Examples

* We deployed the application.
* Jenkins completed the build.
* The server restarted automatically.
* The issue occurred yesterday.

---

# Time Words

Used with:

* Yesterday
* Last week
* Last month
* Last year
* Ago

### Examples

* I completed the task yesterday.

# 2. Past Continuous Tense

## Usage

Used for an action that was in progress at a specific time in the past.

---

## Formula

```text
Subject + was/were + Verb+ing
```

---

# Examples

* I was studying.
* He was working.
* They were playing cricket.

---

# Was vs Were

### Was

Used with:

* I
* He
* She
* It

### Were

Used with:

* You
* We
* They

---

# Examples

* I was sleeping.
* He was working.
* They were waiting.

---

# Negative

```text
Subject + was/were not + Verb+ing
```

### Examples

* I was not studying.
* They were not working.

---

# Questions

```text
Was/Were + Subject + Verb+ing ?
```

### Examples

* Were you working?
* Was he sleeping?

---

# Office Examples

* I was attending a meeting at 10 AM.
* We were discussing the issue.

---

# DevOps Examples

* I was monitoring the server.
* The team was deploying the release.
* We were troubleshooting the issue.

---

# Special Use

Two actions in the past:

### Example

* I was working when the manager called.

* We were deploying when the server crashed.

---

# 3. Past Perfect Tense

## Usage

Used when one action happened before another action in the past.

---

## Formula

```text
Subject + had + V3
```

---

# Examples

* I had completed the work before lunch.
* We had deployed the application before testing started.
* She had left before I arrived.

---

# Understanding the Sequence

### Example

* I arrived.
* She had left.

Which happened first?

✅ She left first.

✅ I arrived later.

---

# Negative

```text
Subject + had not + V3
```

### Examples

* I had not completed the task.
* They had not deployed the release.

---

# Questions

```text
Had + Subject + V3 ?
```

### Examples

* Had you completed the task?
* Had they deployed the application?

---

# Office Examples

* The manager had approved the request before the meeting.
* We had completed testing before deployment.

---

# DevOps Examples

* We had fixed the issue before the outage occurred.
* Jenkins had completed the build before deployment started.

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ I had went.

✅ I had gone.

---

❌ We had did the work.

✅ We had done the work.

---

# 4. Past Perfect Continuous Tense

## Usage

Used for an action that continued for some time before another action in the past.

---

## Formula

```text
Subject + had been + Verb+ing
```

---

# Examples

* I had been studying for two hours before dinner.
* We had been waiting for an hour before the meeting started.
* He had been working since morning before he took a break.

---

# Since vs For

### Since

Starting Point

* Since Monday
* Since 2025
* Since morning

### For

Duration

* For 2 hours
* For 5 days
* For 3 months

---

# Examples

* I had been learning English for six months before joining the course.
* We had been monitoring the servers for two hours before the incident occurred.

---

# Negative

```text
Subject + had not been + Verb+ing
```

### Examples

* I had not been studying regularly.
* They had not been working on the issue.

---

# Questions

```text
Had + Subject + been + Verb+ing ?
```

### Examples

* Had you been studying?
* Had they been working?

---

# Office Examples

* We had been preparing the report for two days before submission.
* The team had been discussing the issue before the manager joined.

---

# DevOps Examples

* We had been monitoring the application before the outage happened.
* The engineers had been troubleshooting the issue for hours.

---

# Comparison of All Past Tenses

## Simple Past

* I worked yesterday.

(Completed action)

---

## Past Continuous

* I was working at 10 AM.

(Action in progress)

---

## Past Perfect

* I had completed the work before lunch.

(Action completed before another action)

---

## Past Perfect Continuous

* I had been working for two hours before lunch.

(Action continuing before another action)

---

# Key Points

✅ Simple Past = Completed Past Action

✅ Past Continuous = Action in Progress in Past

✅ Past Perfect = Action Completed Before Another Past Action

✅ Past Perfect Continuous = Action Continuing Before Another Past Action

✅ Use V2 in Simple Past

✅ Use V3 with Had

✅ Very useful for Incident Reports, Root Cause Analysis (RCA), Project Discussions, and Interviews.

### DevOps Examples

* The deployment failed yesterday.
* We were troubleshooting the issue.
* We had fixed the bug before production deployment.
* We had been monitoring the servers before the outage occurred.
