# Future Tenses

## What is Future Tense?

Future Tense is used to talk about actions that will happen in the future.

### Hindi Meaning

Future Tense का उपयोग उन कार्यों के लिए किया जाता है जो भविष्य में होंगे।

### Marathi Meaning

भविष्यात होणाऱ्या क्रिया सांगण्यासाठी Future Tense वापरला जातो.

---

# Types of Future Tenses

There are four Future Tenses:

1. Simple Future Tense
2. Future Continuous Tense
3. Future Perfect Tense
4. Future Perfect Continuous Tense

---

# Future Tense Overview

| Tense                     | Usage                                 |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Simple Future             | Future action                         |
| Future Continuous         | Action in progress at a future time   |
| Future Perfect            | Action completed before a future time |
| Future Perfect Continuous | Action continuing up to a future time |

---

# 1. Simple Future Tense

## Usage

Used for:

- Future plans
- Predictions
- Promises
- Decisions

---

## Formula

### Positive

```text
Subject + will + V1
```

---

## Examples

- I will learn English.
- He will join the meeting.
- We will deploy the application.

---

# Negative

## Formula

```text
Subject + will not + V1
```

### Examples

- I will not go tomorrow.
- He will not attend the meeting.
- We will not delay the release.

---

# Questions

## Formula

```text
Will + Subject + V1 ?
```

### Examples

- Will you join the meeting?
- Will they deploy the application?
- Will she call you?

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ I will goes.

✅ I will go.

---

❌ He will works.

✅ He will work.

---

# Office Examples

- I will send the report.
- We will complete the task tomorrow.
- The manager will review the document.

---

# DevOps Examples

- We will deploy the application tonight.
- The team will upgrade the server.
- Jenkins will run the pipeline automatically.

---

# Time Words

Used with:

- Tomorrow
- Next week
- Next month
- Soon
- Later

### Examples

- I will call you tomorrow.
- We will deploy next week.

---

# 2. Future Continuous Tense

## Usage

Used for an action that will be in progress at a specific future time.

---

## Formula

```text
Subject + will be + Verb+ing
```

---

# Examples

- I will be studying at 8 PM.
- He will be working tomorrow.
- They will be travelling next week.

---

# Negative

```text
Subject + will not be + Verb+ing
```

### Examples

- I will not be attending the meeting.
- We will not be working on Sunday.

---

# Questions

```text
Will + Subject + be + Verb+ing ?
```

### Examples

- Will you be joining the meeting?
- Will they be deploying today?

---

# Office Examples

- I will be attending a meeting at 3 PM.
- We will be discussing the project tomorrow.

---

# DevOps Examples

- We will be deploying the release tonight.
- The team will be monitoring the servers.
- I will be troubleshooting the issue.

---

# Future Time Examples

- At 8 PM
- Tomorrow morning
- Next Monday

### Examples

- I will be working at 10 AM tomorrow.
- We will be deploying at midnight.

---

# 3. Future Perfect Tense

## Usage

Used for an action that will be completed before a certain time in the future.

---

## Formula

```text
Subject + will have + V3
```

---

# Examples

- I will have completed the task by 5 PM.
- We will have deployed the application by tonight.
- She will have finished the report by tomorrow.

---

# Understanding the Concept

### Example

Current Time → Work Running

Future Time → Work Finished

Example:

- I will have completed the task by 5 PM.

Meaning:

Before 5 PM, task will be finished.

---

# Negative

```text
Subject + will not have + V3
```

### Examples

- I will not have completed the work by noon.
- They will not have finished testing.

---

# Questions

```text
Will + Subject + have + V3 ?
```

### Examples

- Will you have completed the task?
- Will they have deployed the release?

---

# Office Examples

- I will have submitted the report by evening.
- The team will have completed testing by Friday.

---

# DevOps Examples

- We will have deployed the application by midnight.
- The migration will have finished by tomorrow morning.
- The engineers will have completed the upgrade.

---

# Time Words

Used with:

- By
- Before
- By tomorrow
- By next week

### Examples

- I will have completed it by tomorrow.
- We will have fixed the issue before deployment.

---

# 4. Future Perfect Continuous Tense

## Usage

Used for an action that will continue up to a certain point in the future.

---

## Formula

```text
Subject + will have been + Verb+ing
```

---

# Examples

- I will have been studying for two hours by 8 PM.
- We will have been working for six months by December.
- He will have been living here for ten years by next year.

---

# Since vs For

## Since

Starting Point

- Since Monday
- Since 2025
- Since January

---

## For

Duration

- For 2 hours
- For 5 days
- For 3 months

---

# Examples

- I will have been working here for three years by next month.
- We will have been learning English for one year by December.

---

# Negative

```text
Subject + will not have been + Verb+ing
```

### Examples

- I will not have been studying long enough.
- They will not have been working for a year.

---

# Questions

```text
Will + Subject + have been + Verb+ing ?
```

### Examples

- Will you have been working here for two years?
- Will they have been studying for six months?

---

# Office Examples

- I will have been working in Infosys for two years by December.
- We will have been supporting this project for six months.

---

# DevOps Examples

- We will have been maintaining this infrastructure for three years.
- The team will have been monitoring the application for months.

---

# Comparison of All Future Tenses

## Simple Future

- I will work tomorrow.

(Future action)

---

## Future Continuous

- I will be working tomorrow.

(Action in progress in future)

---

## Future Perfect

- I will have completed the work by tomorrow.

(Action completed before future time)

---

## Future Perfect Continuous

- I will have been working for two hours by 5 PM.

(Action continuing until future time)

---

# Common English Mistakes

❌ I will goes tomorrow.

✅ I will go tomorrow.

---

❌ He will be work.

✅ He will be working.

---

❌ We will have complete the task.

✅ We will have completed the task.

---

❌ I will have been work for two years.

✅ I will have been working for two years.

---

# Key Points

✅ Simple Future = Future Action

✅ Future Continuous = Future Ongoing Action

✅ Future Perfect = Future Completed Action

✅ Future Perfect Continuous = Future Ongoing Duration

✅ Use "will" + V1

✅ Use "will have" + V3

✅ Use "will be" + Ving

✅ Use "will have been" + Ving

✅ Future Tenses are commonly used in Planning, Meetings, Roadmaps, Sprint Discussions, Release Planning, and Career Goals.
