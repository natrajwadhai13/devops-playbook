# Question Formation

## What is Question Formation?

Question Formation means creating questions correctly in English.

Questions are used to:

- Ask information
- Request help
- Confirm details
- Start conversations
- Participate in meetings

---

## Hindi Meaning

सही तरीके से प्रश्न (Questions) बनाना Question Formation कहलाता है।

---

## Marathi Meaning

इंग्रजीमध्ये योग्य प्रकारे प्रश्न तयार करण्याला Question Formation म्हणतात.

---

# Types of Questions

There are two main types:

1. Yes/No Questions
2. WH Questions

---

# 1. Yes/No Questions

These questions can be answered with:

- Yes
- No

---

## Formula

```text
Helping Verb + Subject + Main Verb ?
```

---

## Examples

- Are you a DevOps Engineer?
- Is he working?
- Do you speak English?
- Have you completed the task?

---

## Answers

- Yes, I am.
- No, I am not.
- Yes, I do.
- No, I haven't.

---

# Questions with BE Verbs

## Am / Is / Are

### Statement

- You are a student.

### Question

- Are you a student?

---

### Statement

- He is working.

### Question

- Is he working?

---

### Statement

- They are ready.

### Question

- Are they ready?

---

# Questions with DO / DOES

Used in Simple Present Tense.

---

## Formula

```text
Do/Does + Subject + V1 ?
```

---

### Examples

- Do you work in Infosys?
- Does he play cricket?
- Does she speak English?

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ Does he works here?

✅ Does he work here?

---

❌ Do you knows him?

✅ Do you know him?

---

# Questions with DID

Used in Simple Past Tense.

---

## Formula

```text
Did + Subject + V1 ?
```

---

### Examples

- Did you attend the meeting?
- Did they deploy the application?
- Did he call you?

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ Did you completed the task?

✅ Did you complete the task?

---

# Questions with HAVE / HAS

Used in Present Perfect Tense.

---

## Formula

```text
Have/Has + Subject + V3 ?
```

---

### Examples

- Have you completed the task?
- Has he sent the email?
- Have they deployed the release?

---

# Questions with MODAL VERBS

---

## Formula

```text
Modal Verb + Subject + V1 ?
```

---

### Examples

- Can you help me?
- Could you review this?
- Should we deploy today?
- Will you attend the meeting?

---

# WH Questions

WH Questions ask for specific information.

---

# List of WH Words

| Word  | Meaning |
| ----- | ------- |
| What  | Kya     |
| Where | Kahan   |
| When  | Kab     |
| Why   | Kyon    |
| Who   | Kaun    |
| Whom  | Kise    |
| Whose | Kiska   |
| Which | Kaunsa  |
| How   | Kaise   |

---

# 1. WHAT

Used for things or information.

---

## Examples

- What is your name?
- What are you doing?
- What happened?

---

## Office Examples

- What is the issue?
- What is the deployment plan?
- What changes were made?

---

# 2. WHERE

Used for places or locations.

---

## Examples

- Where do you live?
- Where is the office?
- Where is the server located?

---

## DevOps Examples

- Where are the logs stored?
- Where is the Kubernetes cluster?

---

# 3. WHEN

Used for time.

---

## Examples

- When will you join?
- When is the meeting?
- When was the issue reported?

---

# 4. WHY

Used for reasons.

---

## Examples

- Why are you late?
- Why did the deployment fail?
- Why was the server restarted?

---

# 5. WHO

Used for people.

---

## Examples

- Who is your manager?
- Who completed the deployment?
- Who approved the request?

---

# 6. WHOM

Used for the object (formal English).

---

## Examples

- Whom did you call?
- Whom should I contact?

---

### Note

In modern English:

- Who did you call?

is more common.

---

# 7. WHOSE

Used for ownership.

---

## Examples

- Whose laptop is this?
- Whose project is this?

---

# 8. WHICH

Used when selecting from options.

---

## Examples

- Which server is down?
- Which application failed?
- Which team owns this service?

---

# 9. HOW

Used to ask about manner or condition.

---

## Examples

- How are you?
- How does this work?
- How was the deployment?

---

# HOW + Other Forms

---

## How Much

Used for uncountable nouns.

### Examples

- How much work?

---

## How Many

Used for countable nouns.

### Examples

- How many servers?
- How many engineers?

---

## How Long

Used for duration.

### Examples

- How long have you worked here?

---

## How Often

Used for frequency.

### Examples

- How often do you exercise?
- How often do you deploy?

---

## How Far

Used for distance.

### Examples

- How far is the office?

---

## How Old

Used for age.

### Examples

- How old are you?

# Key Points

✅ Questions usually start with a Helping Verb or WH Word.

✅ Use Do/Does for Present.

✅ Use Did for Past.

✅ Use Have/Has for Present Perfect.

✅ Use Modal Verbs for polite questions.

✅ WH Questions ask for information.

✅ Question Formation is essential for Speaking, Interviews, Meetings, and Daily Communication.

✅ Good English speakers ask good questions, not just give good answers.
