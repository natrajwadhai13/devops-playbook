# Direct and Indirect Speech

## What is Speech?

Speech means reporting what someone said.

There are two ways to report speech:

1. Direct Speech
2. Indirect Speech (Reported Speech)

---

## Hindi Meaning

किसी के बोले हुए शब्दों को बताने के दो तरीके होते हैं:

- Direct Speech
- Indirect Speech

---

## Marathi Meaning

कोणीतरी बोललेले शब्द सांगण्याचे दोन प्रकार आहेत:

- Direct Speech
- Indirect Speech

---

# 1. Direct Speech

Direct Speech means repeating the exact words spoken by a person.

### Structure

```text id="f3a9r2"
Subject + Reporting Verb + "Exact Words"
```

---

## Examples

### Direct

Ram said, "I am happy."

### Meaning

Ram ने जो शब्द बोले वही बताए गए हैं।

---

### More Examples

- She said, "I am busy."
- He said, "I work in Infosys."
- The manager said, "The meeting starts at 10 AM."

---

# 2. Indirect Speech

Indirect Speech means reporting the meaning of what someone said, without using the exact words.

### Structure

```text id="j8m4s1"
Subject + Reporting Verb + That + Reported Speech
```

---

## Examples

### Direct

Ram said, "I am happy."

### Indirect

Ram said that he was happy.

---

### Direct

She said, "I am busy."

### Indirect

She said that she was busy.

---

# Why Do We Use Indirect Speech?

Used in:

- Meetings
- Office Communication
- Reporting
- Interviews
- Documentation

---

## Example

Manager:

"We will deploy tonight."

Reported:

The manager said that they would deploy that night.

---

# Important Rule

When the Reporting Verb is in the Past Tense (said, told), the tense usually changes.

This is called **Backshifting**.

---

# Tense Changes

## Simple Present → Simple Past

### Direct

He said, "I work in Infosys."

### Indirect

He said that he worked in Infosys.

---

## Present Continuous → Past Continuous

### Direct

She said, "I am studying."

### Indirect

She said that she was studying.

---

## Present Perfect → Past Perfect

### Direct

He said, "I have completed the task."

### Indirect

He said that he had completed the task.

---

## Simple Past → Past Perfect

### Direct

She said, "I finished the work."

### Indirect

She said that she had finished the work.

---

## Will → Would

### Direct

He said, "I will call you."

### Indirect

He said that he would call me.

---

## Can → Could

### Direct

She said, "I can help you."

### Indirect

She said that she could help me.

---

## May → Might

### Direct

He said, "I may come."

### Indirect

He said that he might come.

---

# Tense Change Table

| Direct Speech      | Indirect Speech |
| ------------------ | --------------- |
| Present Simple     | Past Simple     |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous |
| Present Perfect    | Past Perfect    |
| Past Simple        | Past Perfect    |
| Will               | Would           |
| Can                | Could           |

# SAID vs TOLD

---

## Said

No object immediately after it.

### Example

- He said that he was busy.

---

## Told

Must have a person after it.

### Example

- He told me that he was busy.

---

# Examples

### Correct

- She said that she was happy.
- She told me that she was happy.

---

### Incorrect

❌ She told that she was happy.

✅ She told me that she was happy.

# Key Points

✅ Direct Speech = Exact Words

✅ Indirect Speech = Reported Meaning

✅ Said → Usually followed by "that"

✅ Told → Must have a person (me, us, him, her)

✅ Will → Would

✅ Can → Could

✅ Present → Past

✅ Used heavily in:

- Meetings
- Incident Reports
- Emails
- Status Updates
- Interviews

✅ Professional communication often uses Indirect Speech instead of Direct Speech.
