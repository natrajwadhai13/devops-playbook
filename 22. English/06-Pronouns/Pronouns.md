# Pronouns

## What is a Pronoun?

A Pronoun is a word used instead of a Noun.

Pronouns help us avoid repeating the same noun again and again.

### Example

❌ Natraj is a DevOps Engineer. Natraj works in Infosys. Natraj manages servers.

✅ Natraj is a DevOps Engineer. He works in Infosys. He manages servers.

---

## Hindi Meaning

जो शब्द Noun की जगह उपयोग किया जाता है उसे Pronoun कहते हैं।

---

## Marathi Meaning

Noun च्या जागी वापरल्या जाणाऱ्या शब्दाला Pronoun म्हणतात.

---

# Why Do We Use Pronouns?

Without pronouns, sentences become repetitive.

### Example

❌ Ram is a student. Ram goes to college. Ram studies English.

✅ Ram is a student. He goes to college. He studies English.

---

# 1. Personal Pronouns

Used as the subject of a sentence.

## Subject Pronouns

| Person      | Pronoun |
| ----------- | ------- |
| मी          | I       |
| तू / तुम्ही | You     |
| तो          | He      |
| ती          | She     |
| ते (वस्तू)  | It      |
| आपण / आम्ही | We      |
| ते          | They    |

---

## Examples

- I am a DevOps Engineer.
- You are my friend.
- He works in Infosys.
- She is a manager.
- It is a server.
- We are learning English.
- They are engineers.

---

# Subject Pronoun Formula

```text
Subject Pronoun + Verb
```

### Examples

- I work.
- He works.
- They work.

---

# 2. Object Pronouns

Used as the object of a sentence.

| Subject | Object |
| ------- | ------ |
| I       | Me     |
| You     | You    |
| He      | Him    |
| She     | Her    |
| It      | It     |
| We      | Us     |
| They    | Them   |

---

## Examples

- Please call me.
- I know him.
- I met her yesterday.
- The manager called us.
- I helped them.

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ He helped I.

✅ He helped me.

---

❌ Please call we.

✅ Please call us.

---

# 3. Possessive Adjectives

Used before a noun.

| Pronoun | Possessive Adjective |
| ------- | -------------------- |
| I       | My                   |
| You     | Your                 |
| He      | His                  |
| She     | Her                  |
| It      | Its                  |
| We      | Our                  |
| They    | Their                |

---

## Examples

- My laptop
- Your project
- His server
- Her book
- Our team
- Their application

---

## Sentences

- My laptop is new.
- Our deployment was successful.
- Their server is down.

---

# Common Mistake

❌ This is mine laptop.

✅ This is my laptop.

---

# 4. Possessive Pronouns

Used without a noun.

| Pronoun | Possessive Pronoun |
| ------- | ------------------ |
| I       | Mine               |
| You     | Yours              |
| He      | His                |
| She     | Hers               |
| We      | Ours               |
| They    | Theirs             |

---

## Examples

- This laptop is mine.
- That project is yours.
- The success is ours.

---

# Difference

### Possessive Adjective

Needs a noun.

- My laptop
- Your book

### Possessive Pronoun

No noun needed.

- Mine
- Yours

---

# 5. Reflexive Pronouns

Used when the subject and object are the same person.

| Pronoun | Reflexive Pronoun |
| ------- | ----------------- |
| I       | Myself            |
| You     | Yourself          |
| He      | Himself           |
| She     | Herself           |
| It      | Itself            |
| We      | Ourselves         |
| They    | Themselves        |

---

## Examples

- I completed the task myself.
- He fixed the issue himself.
- We prepared ourselves.

---

# Office Examples

- I verified the deployment myself.
- She completed the documentation herself.

---

# 6. Demonstrative Pronouns

Used to point to specific things.

| Pronoun | Usage           |
| ------- | --------------- |
| This    | Near + Singular |
| That    | Far + Singular  |
| These   | Near + Plural   |
| Those   | Far + Plural    |

---

## Examples

- This is my laptop.
- That is our office.
- These are servers.
- Those are containers.

---

# DevOps Examples

- This server is healthy.
- These containers are running.
- Those pipelines failed.

---

# 7. Relative Pronouns

Connect two sentences.

Common Relative Pronouns:

- Who
- Whom
- Which
- That
- Whose

---

## Examples

### Who

Used for people.

- The engineer who fixed the issue is here.

### Which

Used for things.

- The server which failed was restarted.

### That

Used for people and things.

- The laptop that I bought is fast.

---

# 8. Interrogative Pronouns

Used to ask questions.

## Examples

- Who?
- Whom?
- Whose?
- Which?
- What?

---

## Sentences

- Who is your manager?
- What is your name?
- Which server is down?
- Whose laptop is this?

---

# 9. Indefinite Pronouns

Refer to non-specific people or things.

## Examples

- Someone
- Anyone
- Everyone
- Nobody
- Everybody
- Somebody

---

## Sentences

- Someone called me.
- Everyone attended the meeting.
- Nobody knows the answer.

---

---

## Mistake 3

✅ This is my laptop.

---

## Mistake 4

✅ The project is ours.

---

## Mistake 5

❌ He fixed the issue hisself.

✅ He fixed the issue himself.

# Key Points

✅ Pronouns replace Nouns.

✅ Use I as Subject and Me as Object.

✅ Use My before a Noun.

✅ Use Mine without a Noun.

✅ Use Himself, Herself, Myself for self-actions.

✅ Pronouns make sentences shorter and more natural.

✅ Very important for Speaking, Emails, Meetings, Interviews, and Daily Communication.
