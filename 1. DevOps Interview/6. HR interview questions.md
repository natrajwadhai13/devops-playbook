# Q1. HR Question: Tell Me About Yourself

## Answer

This is usually the first question in HR interviews and gives the interviewer an overview of your background.

A good answer should include:

- Your education or experience
- Your current role
- Your key skills
- Your career goals

Example answer:

“My name is **\_\_**. I have completed my education in **\_\_**.

Currently, I am working as **\_\_** where I handle responsibilities related to **\_\_**.

I have experience working with teams, solving problems, and improving processes.

I am looking for opportunities where I can grow professionally and contribute to the organization.”

## Layman Language

This question is simply asking for a **short introduction about yourself**.

You should explain:

- Who you are
- What work you do
- What skills you have
- What you want to achieve in your career

<a id="q2-why-job"></a>

# Q2. HR Question: Why Do You Want This Job

## Answer

The interviewer wants to understand your motivation for applying.

A good answer should mention:

- Interest in the role
- Alignment with your skills
- Opportunity to grow

Example answer:

“I am interested in this role because it matches my skills and experience.

I believe your company provides a good environment for learning and professional growth.

I would like to contribute my skills and grow along with the organization.”

## Layman Language

HR wants to know:

“Why did you apply here?”

You should explain that:

- The role fits your skills
- The company is good
- You want to grow in your career

<a id="q3-hire-you"></a>

# Q3. HR Question: Why Should We Hire You

## Answer

This question tests your confidence and ability to explain your value.

Example answer:

“You should hire me because I am hardworking, responsible, and willing to learn.

I believe my skills and positive attitude will help me contribute effectively to your team.”

## Layman Language

The company wants to know:

“What makes you a good candidate?”

Explain your:

- Skills
- Attitude
- Learning ability

<a id="q4-strengths"></a>

# Q4. HR Question: What Are Your Strengths

## Answer

Your strengths should relate to your professional abilities.

Common strengths include:

- Problem-solving skills
- Teamwork
- Adaptability
- Quick learning ability

Example answer:

“My strengths include strong problem-solving skills, teamwork, and adaptability.

I also focus on learning new skills and improving myself continuously.”

## Layman Language

Strength means **things you are good at**.

Choose strengths that are helpful for your job.

<a id="q5-weakness"></a>

# Q5. HR Question: What Are Your Weaknesses

## Answer

Weakness should be honest but show improvement.

Example answer:

“Sometimes I focus too much on details.

However, I am improving this by managing my time better and focusing on priorities.”

## Layman Language

Weakness means **something you are working to improve**.

Always explain **how you are improving it**.

# Q6. HR Question: What Do You Know About Our Company

## Answer

This question checks whether you researched the company before the interview.

Example answer:

“From my research, your company provides technology solutions and has a strong reputation in the industry.

I am impressed by your innovation and growth.”

## Layman Language

Before the interview:

- Check the company website
- Understand what they do
- Mention something positive

<a id="q10-questions"></a>

# Q10. HR Question: Do You Have Any Questions for Us

## Answer

It is always good to ask 1–2 questions.

Example questions:

- What does a typical day look like in this role?
- What are the opportunities for learning and growth?
- What are the biggest challenges for this team?

## Layman Language

Never say **“No questions”**.

Ask about:

- The role
- The team
- Growth opportunities

# Q11. HR Question: Why Are You Looking for a Job Change

## Answer

This question helps the interviewer understand your motivation for changing jobs.

A good answer should focus on **growth and learning**, not complaints about the previous company.

Example answer:

“I have learned many valuable skills in my current role and gained good experience working with my team.

However, I am now looking for new challenges and opportunities to grow professionally and work with advanced technologies.”

## Layman Language

Do not say negative things about your current company.

Explain that:

- You learned many things
- Now you want **new challenges and growth**
